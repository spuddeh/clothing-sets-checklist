# Clothing Sets Checklist

**Clothing Sets Checklist** is a tool for collectors who want to complete almost all of the unique clothing sets in Cyberpunk 2077 (life path and epilogue sets are intentionally excluded).

📥 **Download:** [Clothing Sets Checklist on Nexus Mods](https://www.nexusmods.com/cyberpunk2077/mods/26829)

## 🚀 Features

* **Set Grouping:** Organizes items by their outfit set (Media, Rocker, Solo, Netrunner, and more).
* **Reactive Proximity Scanner (v2.0.0):** Built on 0-Engine. Reacts the moment you cross into range of an uncollected item and marks it on your HUD. No polling timer, so no CPU cost when you are away.
* **Smart Scanning:** Scans your Inventory and Wardrobe (Transmog) on startup so already-owned items show instantly, and detects items as you loot them.
* **Quest-Fact Gating:** Quest-locked items (MaxTac, Johnny's Shoes, Johnny's Pants) are not flagged before they are accessible.
* **Smart Pause:** Suppressed during loading screens, fast travel, and menus.
* **Set Pin & Lazy Mode:** Standalone waypoint button; optional Lazy Mode adds Teleport, Give Item (save-persistent), and Unstuck.
* **Per-Character Persistence:** Progress is tied to each save file.

## 📋 Requirements

* [Cyber Engine Tweaks](https://www.nexusmods.com/cyberpunk2077/mods/107).
* [0-Engine](https://www.nexusmods.com/cyberpunk2077/mods/27967) (pure CET-only build). New required dependency as of 2.0.0; it has its own requirements listed on its mod page.

## 🎮 Usage

1. Open the CET overlay.
2. The mod scans your inventory and wardrobe; owned items are checked off.
3. Missing items show their location and step-by-step directions.
4. **Need it now?** Enable Lazy Mode to teleport, or use **Give Item** to add it directly to your inventory.
5. Adjust the detection range in Settings (default 50m, adjustable 25m to 100m).

## ⚙️ Settings

* **Show Base ID:** Toggles display of the raw TweakDB ID for each item (useful for console commands).
